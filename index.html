Mother Teresa

The Saint of the Gutters

Mother Teresa devoted her life to caring for the sick and poor. Born in Macedonia in 1910, she went on to found the Missionaries of Charity, which now serves the needy in more than 130 countries.

Her work was recognized around the world, earning her the Nobel Peace Prize in 1979. She lived a life of humility and compassion, putting the needs of others before her own comfort.

One of her most powerful quotes is: “Not all of us can do great things. But we can do small things with great love.”
